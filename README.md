\# HR Analytics Dashboard using Power BI



\## Project Overview



This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition trends and workforce demographics using Power BI. The objective was to identify key factors influencing employee turnover and provide actionable recommendations to improve employee retention and workforce planning.



The dashboard enables stakeholders to monitor workforce health, evaluate attrition patterns across departments and job roles, and make data-driven HR decisions through interactive visualizations and KPI tracking.



\---



\## Business Problem



Employee attrition can significantly impact productivity, hiring costs, employee morale, and overall business performance. Organizations need visibility into workforce trends to understand why employees leave and which employee segments are at higher risk of attrition.



This dashboard was developed to answer key workforce questions such as:



\* What is the overall employee attrition rate?

\* Which departments experience the highest attrition?

\* Which age groups are most likely to leave?

\* Does compensation influence attrition?

\* Which job roles require focused retention efforts?

\## Key Findings



\### Workforce Attrition



\* Overall attrition rate is approximately 14.8%.



\### Department Analysis



\* Finance and HR departments recorded the highest employee attrition.



\### Age Group Analysis



\* Employees aged 30–39 experienced the highest attrition levels.



\### Compensation Analysis



\* A significant proportion of employee exits occurred within the high salary band.



\### Job Role Analysis



\* Reporting Analysts and Decision Analytics Associates showed the highest attrition rates.



\---



\## Business Recommendations



\### 1. Improve Retention in High-Attrition Departments



Conduct employee engagement surveys and department-level retention reviews for Finance and HR teams.



\### 2. Focus on Mid-Career Employees



Develop structured career growth plans and internal mobility opportunities for employees aged 30–39.



\### 3. Enhance Employee Experience



Improve work-life balance, recognition programs, learning opportunities, and career development initiatives.



\### 4. Retain Critical Talent



Implement targeted retention programs for Reporting Analysts and Decision Analytics Associates.



\### 5. Establish Continuous Workforce Monitoring



Use the dashboard as a decision-support tool to proactively identify workforce risks and emerging attrition trends.
